Our Beginning

From a Single Sewing Machine

StitchCraft was founded in 2005 by master tailor Rajan Pillai in a modest shop in T. Nagar, Chennai. Armed with inherited skills from three generations of tailors and an unwavering commitment to quality, Rajan began with a single sewing machine and a dream.

Word spread quickly. Clients came for alterations and stayed for custom creations. The shop grew, the team expanded, and by 2012 we had opened our second location. Today, StitchCraft employs 12 skilled artisans and serves thousands of clients annually.

We remain family-run, deeply rooted in our community, and passionate about the transformative power of a perfectly fitted garment.

Behind the Scenes

Inside the Workshop

Before any stitch is placed, hours of preparation go into understanding the client's vision, selecting the right fabric, and drafting a precise pattern. Our workshop is where science meets art — measuring tools alongside embroidery frames, cutting tables beside hand-finishing stations.

Every garment passes through at least four expert hands before reaching you — pattern drafter, cutter, tailor, and finisher. This collaborative process ensures no detail is missed and every seam is flawless.

Community & Ethics

Rooted in Chennai, Committed to People

StitchCraft isn't just a business — it's a community institution. We've been part of T. Nagar's fabric since 2005, employing local artisans, supporting apprenticeship programs, and contributing to the city's rich tailoring heritage.

Our sustainability pledge means we repurpose fabric offcuts, source ethically from certified mills, and avoid fast-fashion shortcuts. When you choose StitchCraft, you're supporting craft, community, and conscience.
